Naan Bread

Description

Soft and pillowy naan is easy to make at home, and it's a world apart from store-bought.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all purpose flour, plus more for rolling
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon instant dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon anise seeds (optional)
  • 3 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • ¾ cup warm water (about 100°F)
  • 2 tablespoons melted salted butter
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh Italian parsley (optional), for serving

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, yeast, salt, and anise seeds.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together yogurt, olive oil, and ¾ cup warm water.
  3. Add the yogurt mixture to the dry ingredients and mix with a fork.
  4. When the dough is about to come together, dust your hands with flour and knead gently into a soft, slightly sticky dough. Add more flour if needed to prevent stickiness.
  5. Lightly oil or spray a clean bowl with nonstick cooking spray (large enough for the dough to double in size).
  6. Transfer the dough to the prepared bowl, cover with plastic wrap or a damp kitchen towel, and let it sit in a warm place for 1 to 1½ hours, or until about doubled in size.
  7. Fill a small bowl with about ½ cup of flour.
  8. Dust a work surface with some flour and place the dough on it.
  9. Sprinkle some flour on top of the dough and on your hands.
  10. Shape the dough into a long rectangle and cut it into 6 equal portions, dusting with more flour as necessary to prevent sticking.
  11. Warm a large cast iron or heavy nonstick pan over medium-high heat until very hot.
  12. Roll one of the dough balls into an oval shape about ⅛-inch thick (approximately 9 x 4 inches) using a rolling pin.
  13. Pick up the dough and flip it back and forth between your hands to remove excess flour.
  14. Gently lay the dough in the dry skillet and cook until the top has air bubbles and the bottom is golden and blackened in spots.
  15. Flip the naan and cook for 1-2 minutes more until the bottom is lightly browned and blistered in spots.
  16. Remove the naan from the skillet and brush it with melted butter.
  17. Place the naan in a tea towel-lined dish to keep warm.
  18. Repeat the process with the remaining naan, adjusting the heat as needed.
  19. Sprinkle with parsley, if desired, and serve the naan warm.
